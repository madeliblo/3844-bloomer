Reflection

The concept of digital writing and how the plethora of technological advancements have helped it evolve over centuries, decades, and even small increments of years is what intrigues me the most. As you can see in my two previous reading responses, all of the readings helped me form a better understanding of the impact in technology and, ultimately, digital writing. Creating content through "Rip, Mix, Burn" is a game-changer in today's world of technology because it enables users everywhere the ability to constantly innovate through one another's content.

Since I already have somewhat of a background in HTML, though not extensive by any means, this assignment was not all that difficult. I believe the most challenging aspect was using a template from HTML5Up and trying to work around the complex HTML and CSS codes. Though you advised us not to make it too difficult, I figured it would be just the right challenge to attempt. At one point, this particular section of reflection was being overlapped by the side navigation bar, and it took me about thirty minutes to scrutinize the coding and realize I had an extra /div attribute that cancelled out the wrapper feature.

Another facet I found a bit challenging was dealing with the CSS since I am not as familiar with it as I am HTML, so most of the attributes were new to me and sometimes a bit confusing. What I found the most interesting about composing in HTML was my personal control over what went on my website and the satisfaction of the actual creation of it.

As I mentioned before, the assigned readings on computing and technological advancements has definitely helped expand my views on digital writing. I guess it is no surprise that people in previous decades would envision their hopes for developments in future technologies in terms of accessibility, but the importance of an all-accessible encyclopedia and the practical needs for transferrable knowledge had never crossed my mind before. Considering these ideas for contributions that have actually formed over the years and the concept of VT-learning, I have come to realize the value and overall essentiality of interdisciplinary knowledge and how the implementation is already making a positive impact for the world.
