Introduction

Hi, I'm Feisty, and this is my remix website. I wouldn't exactly say I'm a novice to digital writing, but I'm most definitely not an expert. Yet, that is. For now, I can be classified as an experienced intermediate.

My first instances of working with HTML took place in middle school when I would edit my Myspace layouts. I found that, to an extent, editing the code was simple and common sense. Basically, I would find words that I knew and changed them to my preferences, like font families and sizing and such, and I learned certain elements that were necessary to the overall coding structure so that I could delete the annoying advertisements from whoever created the original codes.

My first experience working with HTML in college took place last year in Professional Writing with Jen Barton. We created our own advertising websites for vacation homes, and this is where I learned a little bit more in HTML with the help of Lynda. Since then, I have gained even more knowledge this year on the overall concept of writing within digital media in several of my current courses and am on my way to becoming an expert in HTML.

Goals

If I would've taken my classes in somewhat of the correct order, I would have been in this class last year, and my experiences would be a lot different than they are now. In other words, I would be a lot less experienced.

My main interest interconnected with digital media is within technical writing, which involves my primary major of Professional and Technical Writing and my current internship within a division of the Information Technology (IT) department, Network Infratructure & Services (NI&S).

As a technical writing student and intern, I constantly work with digital media through creating and editing online content. Even though I am not a student of science, I work with technology on a daily basis, and this technological aspect of my learning, especially prevalent within my work at NI&S, parallels with my professional writing to form an interdisciplinary knowledge, making myself somewhat of an ideal VT-shaped student.

Though I have gained a lot of experience interning within NI&S, my technical writing journey is far from over. I currently have two more internships lined up over the course of the next year.

Overall, my number one goal is to gain as much technical writing experience as possible so that I am well-qualified for my future career path once I graduate.

VT-Shaped Poem

Though I am not a poet, I wrote the following untitled poem as my own design of the VT-shaped Student.

For us to be properly shaped,
To be ready for what we call the real world,
We need interdisciplinary knowledge:

How effective is a writer
If they cannot synthesize?
What about a scientist
Without the ability to write?

How competent are you
With training in only one discipline?
How qualified are you
Without necessary integration?

As our own president has noted,
"What important problem can be solved
Only by engineers who are trained
Only in engineering, or by poets
Who have mastered only poetry?"

A lack of an integration of knowledge
Is the only thing preventing you
From inventing the future.

I think my poem properly conveys the importance of the VT-shaped concept. Through interdisciplinary knowledge, one is able to be a more diversified, well-rounded student and individual.

Through my sections on Goals and Reflection, I think I effectively demonstrate my own identity as a VT-shaped student through my daily interactions with technology and digital media. So, the purpose of my poem is to hopefully speak with and connect to a completely different audience than those directly related to English. Though it fits perfectly in my case, I think it is more important to try to relate to others, such as Engineering majors, in regards to how they can broaden their horizons and live up to the standards of a VT-shaped Student by expanding their knowledge across different disciplines.

Group Work

Below are the two big projects my group and I conducted concerning the VT-shaped Student.

T-Shaped Podcast

Our "T-Shaped Podcast" explores the T in the VT-shaped model, which focuses on making connections across one's knowledge base and discovering crosscutting skills that exemplifies their interdisciplinary knowledge.

In the podcast, an investigator interviewed me and asked me what types of classes I took; I told him that I took classes that combined liberal arts and technology. By taking both types of classes, I was able to use my skills in liberal arts while learning essential web-creation skills I need for the future.

This project emphasized the disciplinary depth that is achieved through interdisciplinary knowledge and technological literacy. In today's world, technological literacy is a vital part of performing daily rituals, and it is important for one to properly adapt to technology in order to compete with today's society of constant innovation. By talking about my liberal arts and technology classes, I exemplified the T in the VT-shaped model because I am using both in order to succeed.

V-Shaped Video

Our "V-Shaped Video," on the other hand, explores the V in the VT-shaped model, which consists of gaining interdisciplinary knowledge through guided, experiential learning and informal, communal learning. In simpler terms, a student's V consists of learning and gaining experience, through not only classes but also, through internships, volunteering opportunities, and research experiences.

This project emphasized the equal importance of knowledge gained inside and outside of the classroom. Through extremely amateur acting, we were able to convey that while the hands-on experience from an internship is absolutely essential in learning about a profession, the work performed through that internship would likely not be possible without the knowledge one gains from learning inside the classroom. This type of experiential learning is able to give one a sense of community, in which they learn and apply concepts that are not necessarily guided in a class.

Reflection

The concept of digital writing and how the plethora of technological advancements have helped it evolve over centuries, decades, and even small increments of years is what intrigues me the most. All of the assigned readings this semester helped me form a better understanding of the impact in technology and, ultimately, digital writing. Creating content through "Rip, Mix, Burn" is a game-changer in today's world of technology because it enables users everywhere the ability to constantly innovate through one another's content.

The need for something as simple as the VT-shaped Student is something that almost seems like common sense, but I had never put much thought into the concept until taking this course. Of course having knowledge over multiple disciplines, such as technology and simple writing, will make you more qualified in comparison to those who are experienced in only one area of discipline.

However, how many students actually think about this concept when they are entering college and trying to decide upon a major? I know that if I were presented the VT-shaped model at my own freshman orientation, it probably would have made me think deeper into what I wanted my future career to look like. Being one who has a passion for writing and editing and a joy for learning new technologies, it only makes sense that technical writing would be my career path. The fact that the VT-shaped model is a primary facet of the university's orientations today is a huge step in the right direction for helping students discover themselves as VT-shaped individuals.

As I mentioned before, the assigned readings on computing and technological advancements has definitely helped expand my views on digital writing. I guess it is no surprise that people in previous decades would envision their hopes for developments in future technologies in terms of accessibility, but the importance of an all-accessible encyclopedia and the practical needs for transferrable knowledge had never crossed my mind before. Considering these ideas for contributions that have actually formed over the years and the concept of VT-learning, I have come to realize the value and overall essentiality of interdisciplinary knowledge and how the implementation is already making a positive impact for the world.
